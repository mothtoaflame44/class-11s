The Holy Grail and Judas the traitor

The Holy Grail is often identified as the cup or chalice used by Jesus Christ during the Last Supper.
According to Christian tradition, this same cup was later used to collect Jesus' blood at his crucifixion. It is revered as a sacred relic with miraculous powers.


Betrayal for Thirty Pieces of Silver:
According to the Gospels, Judas betrayed Jesus to the chief priests for thirty pieces of silver. He identified Jesus to the soldiers by kissing him in the Garden of Gethsemane.


Motives and Remorse:
The Gospels offer different explanations for Judas's betrayal.
Some suggest he was motivated by greed, while others imply he was influenced by Satan.
After Jesus was condemned, Judas felt remorse and attempted to return the money, but he ultimately took his own life.
